Adobe Acrobat is the default choice for PDF software in many Australian organisations. It works well, it is widely recognised, and procurement teams are familiar with it. But default does not mean best value -- and as Adobe Acrobat subscription costs continue to rise, a growing number of Australian businesses and government agencies are evaluating alternatives.

This guide compares the strongest Adobe Acrobat alternatives available in Australia, with verified AUD pricing and an honest assessment of where each product fits.

## Why Look Beyond Adobe Acrobat?

The core issue is cost. Adobe Acrobat Pro for teams is listed at A$419.89 per user per year on adobe.com/au (verified March 2026). Adobe Acrobat Standard for teams costs A$323.88 per user per year. These are subscription fees -- you pay every year, and if you stop paying, you lose access to the software.

For a single user, that may be acceptable. For a team of 10 or more, the three-year cost adds up quickly:

- Adobe Acrobat Pro for teams, 10 users, 3 years: **A$12,597**
- Adobe Acrobat Standard for teams, 10 users, 3 years: **A$9,718**

There is no perpetual licence option for Adobe Acrobat. You rent the software indefinitely.

The second issue is feature overlap. Many organisations pay for Adobe Acrobat Pro when Standard would suffice -- or when an alternative product covers their needs at a fraction of the cost. Paying for capabilities you do not use is a common and avoidable expense.

## The Best Adobe Acrobat Alternatives in Australia

### Tungsten Power PDF Advanced -- A$261 inc GST (one-time)

Power PDF Advanced is the strongest direct alternative to Adobe Acrobat Pro for Australian organisations. It matches Acrobat's core capabilities -- PDF editing, conversion, form filling, e-signatures, redaction, Bates numbering, batch processing, and SharePoint integration -- at a fundamentally different price point.

The key difference is licensing. Power PDF Advanced is a perpetual licence. You pay A$261 per user once and own the software permanently. No annual renewals, no subscription fees, no forced upgrades.

For 10 users over three years:
- **Power PDF Advanced: A$2,610** (one-time)
- **Adobe Acrobat Pro: A$12,597** (subscription)

Power PDF is deployed in government agencies across Australia and is supported locally by [Voice Recognition Australia](https://www.voicerecognition.com.au), which has been providing enterprise software deployment and support for over 25 years.

Power PDF Standard (A$188 inc GST) is available for users who need core editing without enterprise features. A Mac edition is also available.

### Foxit PDF Editor

Foxit is a well-established alternative to Adobe Acrobat with a strong feature set including PDF editing, annotations, form design, e-signatures, and cloud collaboration. The Foxit PDF reader is free and widely used.

Foxit has moved to subscription pricing for its editor product. It is typically cheaper than Adobe Acrobat on a per-user basis, but more expensive than Power PDF over time since Power PDF is a one-time purchase.

Foxit is a reasonable choice for organisations that prefer cloud-first workflows and are comfortable with ongoing subscription costs.

### Nitro PDF Pro

Nitro PDF targets the business market with a polished interface and good Microsoft Office integration. The product covers PDF editing, conversion, digital signatures, and document analytics.

Nitro also uses subscription pricing. It positions itself as a modern alternative to Adobe Acrobat for teams, with competitive per-user pricing. However, like all subscription products, the cost accumulates over time.

### PDF-XChange Editor

PDF-XChange is a Windows-only PDF editor with a generous free tier and a reasonably priced paid version. The editor includes text editing, annotations, OCR, stamps, and form filling. It is popular with individual users and small teams who want capable PDF editing software without the cost of Adobe Acrobat.

PDF-XChange is less suited to enterprise deployment -- it lacks the DMS integrations and batch processing capabilities that larger organisations typically need.

### Free Alternatives

For basic PDF tasks, several free options exist. Adobe Acrobat Reader (free) handles viewing, annotations, and form filling but does not include editing. Browser-based tools like Sejda and Smallpdf cover merging, splitting, and light editing with usage limits. LibreOffice Draw can open and make basic edits to PDF files.

Free tools are adequate for occasional personal use. They are not a realistic replacement for Adobe Acrobat in a business or government environment.

## Making the Switch

Migrating from Adobe Acrobat to an alternative product is less disruptive than most organisations expect. PDF is an open standard -- all compliant editors read and write the same files. The core workflows (editing, converting, signing, redacting) transfer directly.

The practical steps are straightforward: evaluate your current Adobe Acrobat usage to confirm which features your team actually uses, trial the alternative product in your environment, and deploy. Most teams are fully operational within a day.
