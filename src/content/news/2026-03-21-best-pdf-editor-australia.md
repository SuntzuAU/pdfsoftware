Choosing the right PDF editor depends on what you actually need it to do. Some people just want to fill in a form or add a signature. Others need advanced redaction, batch processing, and integration with document management systems. The range of options in Australia spans from completely free online PDF editors through to enterprise-grade desktop software -- and the pricing differences are significant.

This guide compares the best PDF editors available in Australia in 2026, covering both paid desktop software and free alternatives, so you can find the right fit without overpaying.

## What to Look for in a PDF Editor

Before comparing specific products, it helps to know which features actually matter for your workflow. A basic PDF editor handles text editing, annotations, form filling, and digital signatures. More advanced PDF editing software adds redaction, Bates numbering, OCR text recognition for scanned documents, batch processing, and integration with platforms like SharePoint and Microsoft 365.

If you work in government, legal, or compliance environments, features like professional redaction and audit trails are not optional -- they are requirements. If you just need to edit the occasional PDF at home, a free PDF editor will likely be enough.

## The Best Paid PDF Editors in Australia

### Tungsten Power PDF Advanced -- A$261 inc GST (one-time)

Power PDF Advanced is our top recommendation for Australian business and government users. It is a perpetual licence -- you pay once and own it permanently with no annual subscription fees. The feature set covers the core capabilities most organisations need: full PDF editing, conversion to and from Word, Excel and PowerPoint, advanced redaction, Bates numbering, e-signatures, batch processing, and native SharePoint and iManage integration.

The interface is deliberately similar to Microsoft Office, which means most users are productive immediately without training. For a team of 10 users, the total cost is A$2,610 once -- compared to A$11,516 or more for three years of Adobe Acrobat Pro subscriptions.

Power PDF Standard (A$188 inc GST) covers individual users who need core PDF editing without the enterprise features. A Mac edition is also available at A$188.

### Adobe Acrobat Pro -- from A$383.88/year per user

Adobe Acrobat remains the most recognised name in PDF software. The Pro edition includes comprehensive editing, conversion, e-signatures via Adobe Sign, and cloud storage integration. The feature set is excellent, and Adobe offers strong cloud and mobile capabilities.

The main consideration for most Australian organisations is cost. Adobe Acrobat is subscription-only -- there is no perpetual licence option. Individuals pay A$383.88 per user per year; businesses pay A$419.88 per user per year for the teams edition with admin features (adobe.com/au, verified March 2026). Over three years for a 10-person team, that ranges from A$11,516 to A$12,598 -- considerably more than Power PDF Advanced for comparable core desktop functionality.

If your organisation already has an Adobe Creative Cloud or enterprise agreement, Acrobat may be bundled at a lower effective price. Otherwise, it is worth running the numbers. See our [full breakdown of Adobe Acrobat alternatives](/news/2026-03-21-adobe-acrobat-alternatives-australia) for a detailed cost comparison.

### Foxit PDF Editor -- subscription

Foxit has a solid reputation as a lighter-weight alternative to Adobe Acrobat. The editor supports PDF editing, annotations, form creation, and collaboration features. Foxit also offers cloud-based editing and a well-regarded free PDF reader.

Foxit uses a subscription model (as of March 2026). Pricing varies by edition and licensing type. It is generally less expensive than Adobe Acrobat but more expensive than Power PDF over a multi-year period, since Power PDF is a one-time purchase.

### Nitro PDF Pro -- subscription

Nitro PDF targets business users with a clean interface and strong Office integration. Features include PDF editing, conversion, e-signatures, and analytics. Like Foxit, Nitro uses subscription pricing (as of March 2026), so the long-term cost comparison may favour perpetual alternatives.

## Free PDF Editor Options

If your needs are basic, several free PDF editors are available. Each comes with limitations, but they handle common tasks adequately.

**Sejda PDF Editor** is a browser-based tool that handles text editing, form filling, annotations, and basic page manipulation. The free tier limits you to three tasks per day and files up to 200 pages.

**PDF-XChange Editor** offers a generous free tier for Windows. It includes text editing, annotations, OCR, and page management. Some advanced features display a watermark in the free version.

**Canva** now includes basic PDF editing. It works well for visually-oriented documents but is generally not suitable for legal or compliance workflows.

**Online PDF editors** like Smallpdf and iLovePDF handle quick tasks such as merging, splitting, compressing, and converting PDFs. They are convenient for one-off jobs but are not designed for regular business use -- and uploading sensitive documents to third-party servers may not meet your organisation's security requirements.
